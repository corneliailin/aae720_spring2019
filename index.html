AAE720 Quantitative and Applied Economics Seminar

Lectures: TH 103 on Mo 4:00 - 5:00pm

Description

The course consists of presentations from industry professionals and academics on various topics related to the field of quantitative and applied economics. Students will also be introduced to effective presentation skills.

This class meets for 60 minutes each week over the spring semester. Students will work on course learning activities for about 3 hours out of classroom for every class period.

There will be one case study presentation.

Announcements
Class Logistics

Course Prerequisites

  • Students are strongly encouraged to have knowledge of econometrics and microeconomic theory.

Learning outcomes

  • Gain an understanding of current industry topics.

  • Master the vocabulary relevant to the topic, understand the economic context for the issue, and pros/cons associated with potential solutions.

  • Develop professional presentation skills through case study presentation.

Textbook

  • The recommended e-book for the course is Handbook of Marketing Analytics, Natalie Mizik, Dominique M. Hanssens. Edward Elgar Publishing; March 2018.

Grading

Final grades will be determined by computing the weighted average of Participation, zyLabs, Final Program (FP), Exam 1 and Exam 2. Final letter grades may be curved upward, but a minimum guarantee is made of an A for 93 or above, AB for 87 or above, B for 80 or above, BC for 75 or above, C for 70 or above, and D for 60 or above.
